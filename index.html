What is Kimchi?

Kimchi is a traditional Korean fermented dish made primarily from napa cabbage and seasoned with chili pepper, garlic, ginger, and salted seafood. It has been a central part of Korean cuisine for centuries.

Originally developed as a method of food preservation, kimchi represents Korean history, climate, and culinary identity.

Baechu Kimchi Recipe

  1. Cut napa cabbage into halves and salt thoroughly.
  2. Let the cabbage rest until softened.
  3. Rinse and drain excess salt.
  4. Prepare seasoning paste with chili powder, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce.
  5. Mix radish strips into the seasoning.
  6. Coat each cabbage leaf with the paste.
  7. Pack kimchi tightly into a container.
  8. Let it ferment at room temperature for 1–2 days.
  9. Store in the refrigerator to slow fermentation.

Other Popular Kimchi Varieties

  • Radish Kimchi (Kkakdugi)

    Crunchy cubed radish kimchi with bold spicy flavor.

  • Cucumber Kimchi
    (Oi Sobagi)

    Fresh cucumber kimchi, lightly fermented and refreshing.

  • Water Kimchi (Dongchimi)

    Mild and tangy kimchi served in a clear, refreshing brine.

  • Young Radish Kimchi (Chonggak Kimchi)

    Ponytail radish kimchi known for its unique shape and texture.

  • Mustard Leaf Kimchi (Gat Kimchi)

    Bold and aromatic kimchi made with Korean mustard leaves.
