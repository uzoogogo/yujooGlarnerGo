Traditional Baechu Kimchi

What is Kimchi?

Kimchi is a traditional Korean fermented dish made primarily from napa cabbage, seasoned with chili pepper, garlic, ginger, and salted seafood.

It originated as a method of food preservation and represents Korean culture and culinary history.

Baechu Kimchi Recipe

  1. Cut napa cabbage and salt it thoroughly.
  2. Let it rest until softened.
  3. Rinse and drain excess salt.
  4. Prepare seasoning paste.
  5. Mix in radish strips.
  6. Coat cabbage leaves evenly.
  7. Pack tightly into a container.
  8. Ferment at room temperature for 1–2 days.
  9. Refrigerate to slow fermentation.

Other Popular Kimchi Varieties

  • Radish Kimchi (Kkakdugi)

    Crunchy cubed radish kimchi with bold spicy flavor.

  • Cucumber Kimchi (Oi Sobagi)

    Fresh cucumber kimchi, lightly fermented.

  • Water Kimchi (Dongchimi)

    Mild and refreshing kimchi in clear brine.

  • Young Radish Kimchi (Chonggak)

    Traditional kimchi made with ponytail radish.

  • Mustard Leaf Kimchi (Gat Kimchi)

    Bold kimchi with a deep, aromatic flavor.
